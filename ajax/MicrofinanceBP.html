Context
During the past years, ML and AI have been largely used to predict and find patterns in large volumes of data.
The banking industry is not left behind in the use of this technology. One of the best known applications is the development of credit scoring models that help predict if a person will be a good payer. For this, a score is assigned that ranks them in 3 levels: high, medium or low risk of non-payment. Then, the best clients are selected and a credit offer is assigned, thus prequalified credits are born.
Banco Pichincha has a Credit Scoring model designed specifically for clients in the microfinance segment (people with a little business with annual sales lower than $100K). Each month, microfinance loan officers receive a list of pre-qualified clients so that they can be managed and deliver. However, the conversion rate of the pre-qualified credits acquisition did not reach the expected level despite the fact that the use of this product saves considerable time for the executive (direct user) and the client (indirect user).
What are the reasons why the conversion of pre-approved credits is not increased? Is the error in a bad prediction of the model or in the credit offer assignment? These are the questions that this research addresses.
