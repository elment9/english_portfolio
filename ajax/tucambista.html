Context
Currency Exchange, a challenge for Peruvians. Although the official currency in Peru is the New Sol, historically the dollar has been the currency that predominates in purchasing and selling products, payment of debts and in savings also. According to the Banking and Insurance Superintendency, people, companies, and organizations exchanged currency up to USD 986 million a day in 2018.
Even though the volume of transactions is large, exchanging currencies can be a great headache for Peruvians because it is time consuming , they expose themselves to robery and the exchange rates of traditional banks are often unfavorable.
Tu cambista is a digital currency exchange house (web site) that allows people and companies to exchange dollars to soles and vice versa at a competitive rate and in a safe, fast and simple way.
Problem that was discovered
- The main problem of clients is the fear to theft or scam which moves from the Currency exchange houses and street currency exchangers to the digital platforms.
Users told that they felt distrust when it comes to starting to use Tu Cambista due to the fact, they feel the company does not provide the enough information to assure that this is a safe and legal digital platform.
- New users don't understand how Tu Cambista works.
- Users need the transaction to have the immediacy and rate of exchange house or a street money exchanger but with the security of a bank.
